What is Wetpour Surfacing?

Wetpour surfacing is a rubberised safety surface made from EPDM rubber, commonly used in playgrounds and recreational areas to provide a cushioned and slip-resistant environment for children to play.

Its composition includes a mixture of EPDM rubber granules bound together with a specialised polyurethane resin, creating a durable and flexible surface. This combination not only offers impact absorption to prevent injuries from falls but also withstands heavy foot traffic and varying weather conditions.

The benefits of wetpour surfacing extend beyond safety; its seamless and smooth finish enables easy maintenance and cleaning, making it a hygienic choice for outdoor play areas.

The customisable design options allow for vibrant colours and patterns to be incorporated, enhancing the aesthetic appeal of the space.

Plus playgrounds, wetpour surfacing is also utilised in sports facilities, water parks, and even animal enclosures due to its versatility and longevity.

Whether it’s creating a safe environment for children to play or ensuring a durable flooring solution for high-traffic areas, wetpour surfacing remains a popular choice among architects and facility managers alike.

Can Wetpour Repair Be Prevented?

Whilst some damage is inevitable, certain preventive measures can help prolong the lifespan of wetpour surfaces. Regular cleaning, maintenance of perimeter bands, and prompt repairs of minor issues can mitigate the need for extensive wetpour repairs.

Regular cleaning plays a crucial role in preventing dirt and debris build-up, which can accelerate wear and tear on wetpour surfaces. As dirt accumulates, it can degrade the surface and compromise its durability over time.
